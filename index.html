⚠ What is DNS Spoofing?

An attacker sitting on the same network intercepts your DNS query and sends a fake reply with a malicious IP — before the real server can respond. Your browser trusts it because DNS has no authentication. You end up on the attacker's fake website thinking it's real.

⚠ What is ARP Poisoning?

The attacker sends fake ARP replies to both the victim and the gateway — without anyone asking. The victim updates its ARP cache thinking the attacker IS the gateway. The gateway thinks the attacker IS the victim. All traffic now flows through the attacker who can read or modify everything — a perfect MITM position.
